Nucigent provides consulting expertise for the design and development of a wide range of information systems for both government and commercial customers. Our skill sets range from working with customers to identify and reﬁne requirements, through the deployment of systems to production. Our speciﬁc areas of expertise include both web and client-server based systems with emphasis on:

System Requirements Deﬁnition

Transform user requirements into system requirements for further deconstruction to the component level for development and testing. The system requirements will focus on any user interface, functional, environmental, security, and performance needs.

System Concept Deﬁnition

Meet with the customer to determine initial needs and formulate a system design concept that can be used to provide further requirements detail.

System Design and Analysis

Further reﬁne the high level design to produce a detailed system design. The design details will i specify the architecture and analyze the performance required to meet the system requirements.

System Security Architecture

Review the overall system requirements for compliance with the security architecture and compliance regulations.

System Workﬂow Analysis and Deﬁnition

Establish new workﬂows or modiﬁcation of existing workﬂows based on the business process requirements

Digital Rights Management (DRM) and other encryption technologies

Determine the levels and types of data encryption required for the system and speciﬁc products for implementation.

Examples of architecture and engineering projects are:
  1. Revamping Lotus Notes client to web-based front ends
  2. Replacing Windows NT with Active Directory
  3. Migrating from Exchange 200x to Exchange 2008
  4. Replacing legacy procurement system with customized COTS software

Technology evaluation and integration with existing architecture

Compare customer requirements against existing technologies. If new technology is needed to meet the requirements, provide the evaluations and tradeoffs of the various available technologies, make acquisition recommendations and plan the integration of the new technology into the existing infrastructure.

Based on corporate IT Governance, new technologies may be evaluated and inserted into the existing infrastructure to implement a new technology direction, i.e., Lotus Notes clients to Java based web front-ends or Exchange Public Folders to SharePoint.

Disaster Recovery planning and implementation

Establish the enterprise level Disaster Recovery (DR)/Continuity of Operations Planning (COOP) to include hardware, software, policies and procedures.

For new or upgraded systems, identify any modiﬁcations to the existing DR strategy. The DR/COOP strategy will be implemented and tested by the engineering staff at the appropriate phases of the project.

Nucigent consultants collaborate with the customer to assess the technological feasibility of the identiﬁed business requirements. Engineering recommendations are made to ensure the application components ﬁt together, performance requirements are met, and the system design meets customer requirements.
